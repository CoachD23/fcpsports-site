## Built for Players Chasing College Basketball

This is not a camp where your son learns to dribble. The FCP Sports Elite Showcase is two days of high-level competitive basketball designed for high school players who want to be seen.

**Dates:** July 17-18 (Friday and Saturday)
**Why these dates:** The NCAA Division I evaluation period runs July 16-19. This is one of the windows when college coaches are actively watching prospects and evaluating talent. We scheduled the showcase inside this period intentionally.

## What We Track

Every player receives an individual evaluation covering:

- **Scoring ability:** Can he create his own shot? Can he score in transition and in the half court?
- **Ball handling under pressure:** Does the ball stay secure against aggressive defenders?
- **Court vision and decision-making:** Does he make the right play, not just the highlight play?
- **Defensive effort and positioning:** Is he a liability or an asset on the defensive end?
- **Athleticism and motor:** How hard does he play when it matters?
- **Coachability:** Does he respond to instruction during live play?

These are the same things college coaches evaluate. We put them on paper for every participant.

## Who Should Attend

This showcase is for serious high school basketball players — grades 9 through 12 — who are actively pursuing college basketball at any level. That includes Division I, Division II, Division III, NAIA, and junior college programs.

If your son is a varsity starter or a high-level JV player who is ready to compete, this is the right camp. If he is still building fundamentals, our [summer day camps](/camps/summer-day-camp/) are a better starting point.

We draw players from Fort Walton Beach, **Destin, Niceville, Navarre, Crestview, Pensacola**, and across the Panhandle. Some travel from Tallahassee and Mobile. The showcase is a regional event with serious competition.

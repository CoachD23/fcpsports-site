## What Kenny Teaches

Kenny Anderson was one of the premier ball handlers and playmakers of his era. His clinics focus on the skills that made him elite:

**Ball Handling at Game Speed:** Not trick dribbling — functional ball handling that works when a defender is in your face. Change-of-pace, hesitation moves, crossovers with purpose. Kenny demonstrates at full speed and then breaks down the mechanics so players understand the "why" behind every move.

**Court Vision and Decision-Making:** The difference between a good player and a great one is not athleticism — it is seeing the play before it happens. Kenny teaches players how to read defenses, find the open man, and make decisions under pressure. This is the hardest skill to teach and the one where an NBA veteran's experience matters most.

**The Mental Game:** Kenny spent 14 seasons in the NBA. He knows what it takes to prepare, compete, and recover at the highest level. He shares that knowledge with your child — how to handle pressure, how to respond to failure, how to stay locked in when the game gets tight. These are lessons that go beyond basketball.

**Scoring Mentality:** When to attack, when to pull up, when to create for a teammate. Kenny teaches players to be aggressive with intelligence — not reckless, not passive.

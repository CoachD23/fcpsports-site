## The Best Way to Spend Winter Break (That Isn't a Screen)

Winter break is long. By December 28th, the Christmas excitement has faded, the toys are old news, and your kid is climbing the walls. Holiday basketball camp gives them three mornings of structured, active basketball in a real facility — and gives you three mornings to yourself.

**Dates:** December 28-30, 2026 (Monday through Wednesday)
**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM (half-day)
**Grades:** 4-8, co-ed
**Price:** $79 for all three days
**Location:** FCP Sports, Fort Walton Beach FL

This camp falls during Okaloosa County winter break (December 21-31). Three half-day sessions that keep your child active without dominating the rest of your holiday plans.

## What to Expect

Holiday camp is built around **fun and fundamentals**. The energy is high, the coaching is real, and the atmosphere is more relaxed than our summer intensives. This is not boot camp — it is basketball camp during a holiday week, and we match the vibe accordingly.

That said, your child will still learn. Every session has a skills focus:

**Day 1 — Ball Handling and Dribbling:** Station-based drills that build coordination and confidence with the ball. Games and competitions mixed in to keep energy up.

**Day 2 — Shooting and Scoring:** Form shooting, layup technique, and short-range scoring moves. We break down the mechanics in a way kids actually understand and remember.

**Day 3 — Scrimmages and Competition:** Everything from Days 1 and 2 gets tested in live games. Coached scrimmages, skills challenges, and awards for effort and improvement.

## Who This Camp Is For

Holiday camp is our **lowest barrier** entry point. It is perfect for:

- Kids who just started playing basketball and want more practice
- Athletes from other sports who want to try basketball in a structured setting
- Players who attend our summer camps and want to stay sharp over break
- Any kid in grades 4-8 who needs something active and fun during winter break

You do not need previous basketball experience. You do not need to be on a team. You just need court shoes and a willingness to move.

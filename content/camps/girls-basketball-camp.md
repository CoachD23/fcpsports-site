## A Basketball Camp Built Around Girls Who Want to Get Better

There is a real, persistent gap on the Emerald Coast: dedicated basketball programming for girls. Rec leagues are co-ed by default. AAU options skew heavily toward boys' teams. School practice is seasonal and limited. And most "summer basketball camps" you find on Google are designed without female athletes specifically in mind — girls show up, get put in a group of mostly boys, and the programming just runs as-is.

FCP Sports built our girls basketball camp because Fort Walton Beach, Destin, Niceville, Navarre, and the surrounding communities have hundreds of female athletes serious about the game and very few places designed to develop them. Our camp answers that gap with structured, year-round programming led by USA Basketball certified coaches — including female coaches — inside the only dedicated basketball facility on the Emerald Coast.

If your daughter wants to make her middle school team, earn a starting spot at Choctawhatchee or Niceville High, develop the skills colleges actually look at, or simply fall in love with the game in an environment where she's not the lone girl in the gym — this is the camp built for her.

## Why a Dedicated Girls Camp Matters

We coach a lot of co-ed weeks, and they work fine. But there's a reason we built dedicated girls programming on top of that. Female athletes — especially in the 10–14 window — develop measurably faster when they train with other girls. The reasons aren't mysterious:

- **More reps in scrimmage situations.** When boys outnumber girls 8-to-2 in a scrimmage group, the ball doesn't move the same way. In dedicated girls sessions, every athlete gets equal touches, equal shot opportunities, and equal defensive reads.
- **Coaching language calibrated to female athletes.** The technical fundamentals are identical. But the cues, the pacing, the way feedback gets delivered — those work better when the coaching staff has experience with how girls learn the game.
- **Peer modeling.** A 12-year-old girl watching a strong 14-year-old girl execute a move will internalize it differently than watching a boy of any age do the same thing. Representation in the gym matters at this age.
- **Confidence to ask questions.** This one comes up constantly from parents. Girls in mixed groups often hold back on questions. In girls-dedicated sessions, the questions come fast — and so does the development.

We're not making any of this up. Watch the difference between a dedicated girls week and a co-ed week side by side and you'll see it in the first 20 minutes.

## What Your Daughter Will Work On

Skill development at FCP Sports follows a structured curriculum, not random drills. Here's the actual content of a girls camp week:

- **Ball handling under pressure** — two-ball drills, pressure dribbling, change-of-direction work that translates directly to game situations
- **Shooting mechanics** — form refinement, off-the-dribble shooting, catch-and-shoot rhythm, and free-throw consistency (the single highest-ROI skill in girls basketball at every level)
- **Footwork** — pivots, jab steps, triple-threat positioning, defensive slides, closeouts
- **Position-aware development** — guards work on creating, posts work on sealing and finishing, every athlete learns to play multiple positions
- **Game IQ** — film review during midday breaks (yes, even at the youth level), spacing concepts, defensive reads
- **Live competition** — coached scrimmages every day with real-time feedback and Friday's competitive challenges

Monday includes a baseline skill assessment. Friday includes a repeat assessment. Parents leave the week with a written breakdown of exactly what improved.

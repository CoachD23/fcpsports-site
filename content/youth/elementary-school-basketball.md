## Why Elementary School Is the Best Time to Start Basketball

The window between Kindergarten and 5th grade is the most important developmental period in an athlete's life — and most parents don't realize it until it's already passed. At FCP Sports, we built our elementary programs around one simple truth: **kids who fall in love with basketball early stay with it longer, develop faster, and have more fun doing it.**

This is not about pressure, rankings, or tryouts. It's about giving your child a sport they'll carry with them for the rest of their life.

## Little Ballers — Kindergarten Through 2nd Grade

Our Little Ballers program is designed for kids ages 5–7 who are learning what a basketball even is. Sessions are 45 minutes of structured play, movement games, and age-appropriate fundamentals. We teach dribbling with two hands before we ever ask for one. We teach how to follow directions before we teach how to set a screen.

**What Little Ballers covers:**
- Coordination and body control through movement games
- Ball-handling basics: bouncing, catching, passing
- Shooting form — muscle memory built right from the start
- Listening skills and sportsmanship
- Making friends and loving the game

Every coach in our Little Ballers program has passed a background check and completed youth development training. Your 5-year-old is in good hands.

## Junior Program — 3rd Through 5th Grade

By 3rd grade, athletes are ready for more structure. The Junior Program introduces real basketball concepts — dribble-drive, defensive positioning, reading the floor — while keeping the environment fun and low-pressure. This is where athletes from Plew Elementary in Niceville, Destin Elementary, Eglin Elementary, and Bob Sikes Elementary in Crestview start building the foundation that will carry them into middle school.

**What the Junior Program covers:**
- Dribbling combinations and ball security under pressure
- Passing: chest pass, bounce pass, overhead pass
- Shooting off the dribble and from set positions
- Basic offensive and defensive concepts
- 3-on-3 and 5-on-5 game play

## What Parents Should Expect

We know you have questions — we get them every week. Here's what to expect when you enroll your child:

**Week 1:** Your child gets a brief skills check-in (informal, not a tryout) so coaches can match them to the right group within the program. Kids think it's just another drill. It is — but it also tells coaches everything they need to know.

**Weeks 2–4:** Fundamentals and routines get established. Your child learns the gym rules, the coach names, and starts building confidence with the ball.

**Ongoing:** Progress updates come from coaches directly. You can ask a coach at pickup or reach out anytime. We're not a drop-off operation — we're a community.

Sessions run after school and on weekends. We have athletes coming directly from Holley-Navarre Primary, Bluewater Elementary in Niceville, Antioch Elementary in Crestview, and Florosa Elementary right here in Fort Walton Beach.

## Why Middle School Is the Make-or-Break Window for Basketball Players

There is a moment — usually somewhere between 6th and 8th grade — when the players who will make their high school team separate from the players who won't. It is not about raw talent. It is about who put in the work when no one made them.

At FCP Sports, we built our Elite Youth program around this reality. Middle school is not too early to train seriously — it is the last window before serious training becomes absolutely necessary.

Research consistently shows that **70% or more of high school players who earn varsity minutes trained outside of school during their middle school years.** The players who rely only on school practice fall behind and rarely catch up.

## The Elite Youth Program: 6th Through 8th Grade

Our Elite Youth program is where the development gets real. Athletes coming from W.C. Pryor Middle, C.W. Ruckel Middle in Niceville, Holley-Navarre Middle, Davidson Middle in Crestview, Clifford Meigs Middle in Shalimar, and Destin Middle all train together in a competitive, structured environment that mirrors what high school coaches demand.

**What Elite Youth training covers:**

- **Ball handling:** Multi-move combinations, speed dribbling, hesitation moves, crossover series under defensive pressure
- **Shooting:** Catch-and-shoot off screens, pull-up off the dribble, free throw consistency, shot chart tracking over time
- **Defense:** Stance, on-ball pressure, help-side positioning, boxing out — the things rec programs rarely teach
- **Basketball IQ:** Reads off screens, ball-movement principles, understanding offensive and defensive schemes
- **Film review:** Athletes see themselves on video. This is the single fastest way to fix bad habits.

High school coaches do not have time to develop raw athletes — they need players who can contribute immediately. That means your child walks into tryouts for Fort Walton Beach High, Choctawhatchee, Niceville, Navarre, or Crestview already knowing how to play, already in condition, and already carrying the habits coaches want to see.

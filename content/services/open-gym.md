## The Only Dedicated Indoor Basketball Open Gym Between Pensacola and Panama City

If you want to play pickup basketball in Fort Walton Beach, your options are limited. Outdoor courts in northwest Florida are largely unusable from May through September — combination of 95-degree heat, afternoon thunderstorms, and sun glare that makes the game miserable. And indoor options? Most are rec centers where basketball competes with pickleball, zumba, and aftercare programs for court time.

FCP Sports open gym is different. This is a **dedicated basketball facility** with a hardwood court, regulation 10-foot hoops, a scoreboard, climate control, and real rims that are tight and true. No sharing with other sports. No waiting for a school function to end.

We are the only venue of this kind between Pensacola and Panama City. If you live on the Emerald Coast and you want to run, this is where it happens.

## How Open Gym Works at FCP Sports

Open gym is unstructured and self-organized, the way pickup basketball is meant to be. Show up, pay your drop-in fee, lace up, and claim your spot. Games are run on a first-come, next-game system — call out your team, win and stay, lose and give up the court.

There is no coach running drills, no curriculum, no pressure. This is basketball in its most natural form. The only structure we provide is court management (keeping games running smoothly) and enforcement of the house rules.

For players enrolled in FCP Sports training programs, open gym is the perfect complement: **train on Tuesday, test it in open gym on Thursday.** That cycle — structured work, then free application — is how skills actually get locked in.

## Who Shows Up: The Open Gym Community

Adult open gym at FCP Sports draws a mix of military personnel from Eglin AFB and Hurlburt Field, local teachers, coaches, former high school and college players, and competitive recreational athletes. Expect legitimate pickup runs — not casual half-court games.

Youth open gym draws players from throughout Okaloosa County: kids from Fort Walton Beach, Niceville, Navarre, Destin, and Crestview who want more court time than school or a single weekly practice provides. Tourists visiting from Destin who play ball are welcome too.

## Open Gym Rules & Court Etiquette

- Court shoes required (no dark-soled shoes that mark the floor)
- No dunking on the rim — grip it and rip it, but no hanging
- Full-court games during prime hours; half-court when attendance is low
- Respect other players — hard fouls are handled by facility staff
- Phones off the court during games
- Spectators welcome in designated seating areas

## Pricing: Drop-In Rates vs. Monthly Passes

Open gym is the **lowest-commitment entry point** to everything FCP Sports offers. You don't have to register for a program, sign a contract, or commit to a season. Pay per visit or buy a monthly pass if you're going to be a regular.

Monthly passes cover unlimited open gym sessions (youth or adult, not combined) and represent significant savings for anyone who comes more than twice a week.

## Why Private Basketball Lessons Accelerate Growth Faster

In a group training session with 8 players, your child gets the coach's attention for roughly one-eighth of the time. In a private lesson, they get 100% of it. That concentration of attention is why private lessons produce faster results — every rep is corrected, every habit is reinforced, and bad mechanics don't get to solidify through repetition.

Private basketball lessons at FCP Sports are for athletes who want to close a specific gap fast: the player who shoots with a hitch, the one who goes right only, the 8th grader who wants to make the high school JV team, or the high schooler preparing to walk on in college. **Precision problems require precision solutions.**

Our coaches have trained players from every high school in Okaloosa County — Choctawhatchee, Niceville, Fort Walton Beach High, Crestview, and South Walton — as well as adults who simply love the game. If your schedule is irregular (as many Eglin AFB and Hurlburt Field families know), we accommodate evening and weekend slots to work around shift work and deployment cycles.

## What a Private Lesson at FCP Sports Looks Like

Here is the breakdown of a standard 60-minute private lesson:

| Time | Segment |
|------|---------|
| 0–10 min | Dynamic warm-up, activation, loose ball handling |
| 10–25 min | Ball handling block — targeted to player's weaknesses |
| 25–40 min | Shooting block — form, spot work, off-the-dribble |
| 40–50 min | Game situations — 1-on-0 moves, live reads |
| 50–60 min | Cool-down, coach feedback, parent debrief |

The final 10 minutes are where lessons differentiate FCP Sports from coaches who simply run drills. We tell parents exactly what we worked on, what improved, and what the focus should be until the next session. You leave knowing what your child's homework is.

## Skills Covered in Private Lessons

Private lessons are fully customized to the athlete, but the most commonly requested focus areas are:

- **Shooting form correction** — fixing the hitch, elbow alignment, follow-through
- **Weak-hand development** — most players are functionally one-handed; we fix that
- **Ball handling under pressure** — dribble combos, change-of-speed, reading defenders
- **Footwork** — pivot technique, jab steps, euro steps, drop steps
- **Post game** — footwork, shot fakes, drop steps for bigger players
- **Guard skills** — pull-up jumper, mid-range, pick-and-roll reads
- **Confidence and mindset** — for players going through a rough patch or facing a big tryout
